WHY DO WE NEED A BRAND?

There is a logical consequence for any organization that fails to develop a compelling brand: It becomes fungible – not a good place to be if you have direct or indirect competitors. A “me too” product or service is harder to differentiate from other similar offers, which in return puts pressure on you to compete in the market mainly through price. Lower pricing, however, cuts your profit margin and starves your company from vital investments which inevitably leads to a loss of your competitive edge.

HOW DOES A BRAND COME TOGETHER?

Building a brand for your business is a multi-step process. More importantly though, the steps need to be in the right order. For example, there is no point in spending any advertising dollars for your organization if you cannot answer convincingly why someone should be interested in you ("Brand Promise"). In addition, without your message looking professionally ("Brand Visuals"), your audience will never take you seriously. Attracting the right type of customers to your business (“like bees to honey”) is an artform and it starts by recognizing the confluence of knowing what to say and how.
